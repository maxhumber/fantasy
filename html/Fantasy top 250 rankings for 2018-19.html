Fantasy top 250 rankings for 2018-19

Kase, Werenski rise after Perry, Jones injuries; rookies Pettersson, Donato have late-round appeal; Rattie joins list on top line with McDavid

by Fantasy Hockey Staff @NHLFantasy / NHL.com

NHL.com's top 250 player rankings are based on past production and expectations for this season in standard Yahoo leagues. For more fantasy coverage, visit NHL.com/Fantasy.

 

MORE RANKS: F (C | LW | RW) | D | G 

PROJECTIONS: Forward points | D-man | Goalie wins

First | Second lines | Power plays | Keeper / dynasty

Sleepers | Deep | Breakouts | Rookies | Bargains

Hits guide | Face-offs | Buyer beware | Cheat sheet

 

Impact of Kucherov, Johnson injuries on Lightning

Tampa Bay Lightning right wing Nikita Kucherov sustained a lower-body injury in their preseason game against the Florida Panthers on Tuesday and did not return. Tyler Johnson (upper body) is uncertain for the start of the season. Kucherov dropped one spot, behind Pittsburgh Penguins center Sidney Crosby, because of this injury uncertainty but is still worth drafting in the early first round until we know more.

Forward Yanni Gourde, who had 64 points (25 goals, 39 assists) as a rookie last season, would be elevated in the lineup if Kucherov and/or Johnson misses any time and has joined the top 150 rankings as a result. Left wing Ondrej Palat, who plays a scarce position and produces hits, would take on added responsibility in the top six.

Impact of Jones injury on Blue Jackets

Columbus Blue Jackets defenseman Seth Jones is expected to miss 4-6 weeks because of a knee injury sustained in the preseason against the Buffalo Sabres on Tuesday. Jones should still be drafted in all leagues but is no longer ranked inside NHL.com's top 100. He's worth stashing on your fantasy bench or an injured reserve spot until he returns, but fantasy owners who have yet to draft should instead target Zach Werenski, who's expected to be ready for the start of the season after offseason shoulder surgery.

Werenski, who's normally paired with Jones at even strength, would be in line to take on first power-play responsibilities with forwards Artemi Panarin, Pierre-Luc Dubois and Cam Atkinson. Panarin (neck) is also dealing with injury concern, and he and goalie Sergei Bobrovsky are potential 2019 unrestricted free agents, adding to the growing uncertainty surrounding the Blue Jackets' fantasy value.

 

Impact of Perry injury on Ducks

Anaheim Ducks right wing Corey Perry is expected to miss five months after having knee surgery Wednesday. He sustained the injury in warmups before Anaheim's game against the Arizona Coyotes on Monday. Perry's absence presents an opportunity for deep sleeper candidate Ondrej Kase, who had 20 goals in 66 games on mostly the third line last season, to play on the top line with center Ryan Getzlaf and left wing Rickard Rakell. Kase or Jakob Silfverberg (potential 2019 UFA) should compete for a first power-play role with Perry and Ryan Kesler (hip) sidelined.

Kase should be targeted ahead of Silfverberg in drafts, but each could be fantasy-relevant this season with the likelihood of increased usage. Another name to track is Ducks rookie forward Troy Terry, who has skated with Getzlaf at times this preseason. Ducks goalie John Gibson, who's now playing behind a lineup minus Perry and Kesler, has dropped behind Penguins goalie Matt Murray in NHL.com's goalie rankings.

Video: How will new teams affect Karlsson and Tavares?

 

Oilers top six taking shape

The Edmonton Oilers have two players to keep an eye on this preseason in right wing Ty Rattie, who is playing on the top line with Connor McDavid and left wing Ryan Nugent-Hopkins, and rookie wing Kailer Yamamoto, who's on the second line with Leon Draisaitl. Rattie and Yamamoto have a chance to stick in their current spots with the Oilers seeking more lineup continuity, making each worthy of a late-round flier or waiver wire pickup.

 

Rookie developments

-- The success of rookie center Elias Pettersson during the preseason is making his even-strength and first power-play linemate, left wing Sven Baertschi, an intriguing deep sleeper. If Pettersson's success carries over, the Canucks top six would have sneaky value. For now, Pettersson is worth reaching for around pick 140 and could be a Calder Trophy finalist.

-- Dual-eligible rookie wing Ryan Donato of the Boston Bruins has solidified a top-nine role this preseason. Donato will especially be fantasy-relevant if he plays on the first power-play unit with forwards Brad Marchand, David Pastrnak, Patrice Bergeron and defenseman Torey Krug and is worth drafting in late rounds.

-- Montreal Canadiens rookie center Jesperi Kotkaniemi continues to make a case for a full-time NHL role. He's currently centering the third line but has a ceiling of leading the Canadiens in scoring if he finds his way into the top six. For now, the 18-year-old is worth monitoring and may be worth drafting late or adding if he makes the team.

-- Philadelphia Flyers rookie goalie Carter Hart is worth monitoring with Michal Neuvirth (undisclosed) and Alex Lyon (lower body) dealing with injuries. Neuvirth and Brian Elliott are potential 2019 UFAs, creating a potential path for Hart to emerge as the starter at any point this season.

-- Dallas Stars rookie left wing Roope Hintz has seen time centering the top line with elite left wing Jamie Benn and right wing Alexander Radulov this preseason. If Dallas breaks up the elite duo of Benn and Tyler Seguin under new coach Jim Montgomery, Hintz could emerge as a sneaky waiver wire add in fantasy.
